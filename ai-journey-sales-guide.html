Internal use only
The core idea in plain English
AI Journey sends abandoned cart and marketing texts on behalf of our merchants. We rely on the merchant to get proper consent from their customers before we send anything. That means the merchant is responsible for collecting consent correctly — but we are also on the hook if we send texts to people who haven't properly agreed to receive them. Different states and provinces have different rules, and some are strict enough that we need to take extra steps before enabling a merchant.

What we need from every merchant — always
✓
What merchants must do
Have a working opt-in checkbox on their checkout or signup form — separate from their terms and conditions
Only send to customers who checked that box
Honor every STOP/unsubscribe request immediately
Keep a record of who consented and when
Make sure their privacy policy mentions SMS marketing
✕
What merchants cannot do
Send to a contact list purchased from a third party or lead-gen service
Assume consent from a past purchase alone — they need an explicit opt-in for marketing texts
Send between 9pm–8am in the recipient's local time (some states are even stricter)
Continue texting anyone who has replied STOP
Use pre-checked boxes or consent bundled into terms and conditions
Always escalate to Legal before enabling AI Journey for these situations
